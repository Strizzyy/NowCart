A Real User's Thursday Evening
Priya wants to cook Paneer Butter Masala tonight. She opens Blinkit, searches "paneer" — 12 results, 4 pack sizes, 3 brands. Picks one. Searches "tomato puree" — out of stock in her preferred brand. Searches again. Then butter, then cream, then spices — one by one. 7 minutes later, she's still building the cart. The food arrives in 10 minutes. The deciding took just as long as the delivery.
1

Cart-building is the hidden bottleneck

Every app makes users search item by item, compare 4–6 variants per product, and manually hunt substitutes when something is OOS. The 10-min delivery window is irrelevant if the cart takes just as long to build.

2

OOS dead-ends drive permanent churn

A user hits an out-of-stock item mid-cart. The app shows nothing. They close the app and open a competitor's. Every dead-end is a permanent trust loss — not a temporary inconvenience.

3

Paradox of choice — no decision layer

6 variants of tomato puree: different brands, weights, and prices. No platform tells you which one fits your recipe or budget. Users freeze, compare, and abandon rather than decide.
